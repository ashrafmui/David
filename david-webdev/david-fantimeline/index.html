GABE NEWELL

‍

1962:

Gabe Logan Newell is born on November 3, 1962 in Seattle, Washington.

‍

1980 - 1983:

Gabe attended Harvard University from 1980 until 1983, where he dropped out to work at Microsoft. He worked there for 13 years.

‍

1996:

Gabe and another Microsoft employee, Mike Harrington, left Microsoft and founded Valve LLC. This is where they developed one of the most pivotal games in the video game scene, "Half-Life".

‍

1998:

"Half-Life" is released. "Half-Life" received acclaim for its graphics, realistic gameplay, and seamless narrative. It won over fifty PC "Game of the Year" awards and is often considered one of the greatest games of all time. It influenced first-person shooters for years after its release; according to IGN, the history of the genre "breaks down pretty cleanly into pre-Half-Life and post-Half-Life eras.
